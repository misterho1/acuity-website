## Patterns worth adopting for Acuity

### 1. IA patterns

**Two dominant IA models on page-1 firms:**
- **By client type first** (Dark Horse): Business / Personal as the primary nav split. Best for SMB-focused firms — reduces decisions, matches how visitors think.
- **By service type first** (Haynie, Tanner, Squire, CMP, Mountain): Tax / Audit / Advisory as primary split. Better for multi-service firms with significant audit/assurance lines.

**6/6 firms use service x industry as a dual axis** somewhere in the IA. Industry pages are universal — they exist for a reason (long-tail SEO + niche credibility). Pick 4–6 verticals that match Acuity's actual book.

**Sweet spot for top-level service buckets: 3–4** (Tanner has 3, CMP has 4 — both feel scannable). Haynie's 18-item flat list is a UX antipattern. Don't replicate.

**Recommendation for Acuity:** Client-type-first nav (Dark Horse pattern) since Acuity is positioned for SMB + individual high-earner clients. Inside each, use 3–4 service buckets, with industry pages as a secondary discovery axis.

### 2. Trust-signal patterns

**Ranked by frequency across 6/6 firms:**

| Signal | Firms using it | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Years-in-business | 5/6 | Mountain Tax is the only one not displaying this — and it shows. Even 5+ years is worth surfacing. |
| Awards / press | 5/6 | "Top 100/200" badges are everywhere. Local awards (CMP's "Best of Northern Utah") perform especially well for Utah SEO. |
| AICPA / professional certs | 4/6 | Table-stakes. CPA badge minimum. |
| Office locations | 4/6 | Multiple locations or even a single anchored address builds local credibility. |
| Team bios with depth | 2/6 (Dark Horse + CMP) | Underused. Dark Horse-style profiles (specialties, hometown, education, direct contact) are a major differentiator. |
| Named client testimonials | 1/6 (CMP) | Most underused signal. CMP's 10+ named testimonials are a huge edge. |
| Client logos | 1/6 (CMP) | Same — high impact, low cost. |
| Quantified outcomes (NPS, retention %) | 1/6 (Dark Horse) | Highest-trust signal. Hardest to fake. |
| Tooling/partner badges | 1/6 (Dark Horse) | QBO/Xero/Gusto badges signal modern-stack fluency. |
| SOC 2 / security certs | 2/6 | Increasingly relevant for advisory-fee SMB clients. |

**Recommendation for Acuity:**
- Must-have: years in business, CPA credentials, at least 1 award/recognition badge, SLC/Utah location
- Differentiator-tier: 5+ named testimonials with photos, deep team bios (Dark Horse pattern), tooling badges, any quantified outcome (retention, response time, refund recovered)
- Skip: vanity stats with no comparison ("600+ professionals" only matters if you have them)

### 3. Lead-capture patterns

**Friction is universally low** — every page-1 firm of the six made it easy to call. None used multi-step forms for general inquiries.

| Mechanic | Firms using | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| Free consultation offer | 5/6 | Standard. Must-have. |
| Phone number elevated to nav/header | 6/6 | Universal. |
| Calendly / inline scheduling | 0/6 | Surprising gap — opportunity for Acuity to differentiate. |
| Pricing transparency | 1/6 (Dark Horse) | Bold differentiator; aligns with "trust" positioning. |
| Lead magnet (downloadable guide, calculator) | 2/6 (Mountain Tax calculators, Haynie tax organizer) | Underused. |
| Tax organizer / file drop-off (existing-client UX) | 4/6 | Existing-client comfort signal. |
| 48-hour response SLA stated | 1/6 (CMP) | Cheap, unique-ish, friction-reducer. |

**Recommendation for Acuity:**
- Embed Calendly (or similar) directly on contact + homepage. Zero of the six do this — it's a fast-mover advantage.
- Offer free consultation prominently (table stakes).
- State a response SLA (24 or 48 hours) — costs nothing, increases conversion.
- Consider a single high-value lead magnet (e.g. "Utah small-business tax checklist" PDF) — only 2/6 firms have any downloadable, and neither's is compelling.
- Pricing page is high-risk / high-reward. If Acuity wants the Dark Horse positioning, do it. If positioning bespoke / advisory, skip.

### 4. Content patterns

| Pattern | Firms using | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Blog / Insights section | 6/6 | Universal; not a differentiator. |
| Visible recent post surfaced on homepage | 3/6 (Tanner, Squire, Dark Horse) | Half do, half don't. Doing it signals freshness. |
| Podcast | 2/6 (Tanner, Dark Horse) | Hard to maintain; skip unless committed. |
| Vlog / video | 1/6 (Dark Horse) | Same. |
| Categorized guides library | 2/6 (Mountain Tax, CMP) | Underused. Static tax guides organized by life-event/business-event are evergreen SEO gold. |
| Financial calculators | 1/6 (Mountain Tax) | Smart — utility content drives backlinks + dwell time. |
| FAQ section on homepage | 2/6 (Haynie, CMP) | Underused. Strong for SEO snippets. |
| "Problems we solve" framing | 1/6 (CMP) | Unique IA pattern — frames services as solutions. |

**Recommendation for Acuity:**
- Blog: yes, but commit to monthly minimum or don't do it (stale dates hurt more than no blog).
- Build 5–10 evergreen guides (PDF or long-form pages) categorized by life-event/business-event — combines SEO + lead magnet.
- Add 2–3 calculators (refund estimator, S-corp savings, etc.) — utility content is rare among competitors.
- Homepage FAQ section with 5–8 questions — easy SEO win, schema-friendly.
- Skip podcast/vlog unless the team genuinely has the bandwidth.

### 5. Local SEO patterns

**Universal among page-1 firms:**
- 5/6 surface multiple Utah city names in nav, footer, or copy.
- 4/6 maintain dedicated per-location landing pages with unique URL slugs (e.g. `/salt-lake-city-cpa-firm/`, `/logan/`).
- 6/6 display phone numbers in headers — schema-friendly NAP setup.
- Reviews integration visible on 3/6 (CMP, Dark Horse, Haynie via FAQ refs).

**Schema markup:** Could not confirm via WebFetch (tool strips JSON-LD). Don't fabricate findings — assume top firms use `LocalBusiness` and `AccountingService` schema and Acuity should as well.

**Recommendation for Acuity:**
- Build location landing pages for every city Acuity serves (SLC, Park City, Provo, Lehi, etc.) — each as its own URL with unique copy and city-specific signals (testimonials from that city, embedded GBP reviews).
- Implement `LocalBusiness` + `AccountingService` schema on home + each location page.
- Surface Google reviews count + star rating on homepage (CMP-style trust signal).
- Embed GBP map on homepage and location pages.
- Use city names in H1s, page titles, meta descriptions on the location pages — none of the six over-optimize, so there's room.

## Anti-patterns (do NOT replicate)

1. **18-item flat service list** (Haynie). Users skip past it.
2. **Stale-dated lead magnets** (Squire's "2020 Tax Organizer"). A dated download signals the firm is asleep.
3. **Generic "values" walls** (Haynie's Trust/Growth/Team/Service block). Performs worse than concrete trust signals like reviews or stats.
4. **Multi-step qualifying forms for new-client inquiries** (Dark Horse uses these for hiring; that's fine; never do it for prospects).
5. **Unnamed testimonials** (Mountain Tax). Hurts more than it helps — looks fabricated.
6. **Blog with no recent posts visible** (CMP's main `/blog/` returned empty during fetch). If the blog appears stale, hide it.
7. **Phone number only on contact page**. Six of six firms put phone in the nav; missing this is leaving conversions on the table.
