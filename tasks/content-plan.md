# Acuity Insights — SEO Content Plan (v1)

> 12 blog post outlines for `/insights/`, targeting Utah-specific tax + small-business search intent.
> Outlines only. **Do not publish before a director reviews each piece for technical accuracy.** The draft language below is suggestive — not Acuity's published voice yet.
> Cadence target: 1 post / month for the first year. Most of the SEO value is in evergreen, well-internally-linked posts — not in volume.

---

## Strategy notes

**Keyword research approach:** Each post targets a primary Utah-modified long-tail keyword + 2–3 supporting variations. Acuity's domain authority is starting fresh on the new site; long-tail (low-volume, low-competition) keywords convert faster than head terms.

**Geo strategy:** Every post mentions at least one Utah city/county naturally — Ogden, South Ogden, St. George primary; Salt Lake City, Park City, Provo, Lehi as secondary "we serve" markers when the topic supports it. Never stuff.

**Internal linking strategy:** Every post links to (a) the relevant service detail page, (b) the relevant location page, (c) at least one other Insights post once 3+ are published. The Insights section's job is to push authority to `/business/*` and `/personal/*`.

**Avoid:** evergreen tax-tip-listicles that look like every other CPA blog. The angle worth winning is *Utah-specific* + *advisory framing* (not preparer framing).

**Topic clusters:** Posts grouped into three clusters to support pillar-page SEO:
1. **Business owner advisory** (5 posts) — links to `/business/`
2. **Personal high-earner planning** (4 posts) — links to `/personal/`
3. **Utah-specific local content** (3 posts) — links to location pages

## What this plan deliberately omits

- **General tax-tips listicles** ("10 tax deductions for small business"). These rank for nothing competitive and look like every other CPA blog.
- **Year-specific rate-table posts** ("2026 tax brackets explained"). Stale within 12 months and require constant rewriting.
- **Pure news commentary** ("New tax law passed"). Useful for client emails, dilutive for SEO unless extraordinary depth is added.
- **Clickbait headlines** ("This ONE WEIRD trick the IRS doesn't want you to know"). Off-brand for an established advisory firm.

The plan favors a smaller number of *deeper, evergreen* posts that win long-tail Utah-specific searches over years, not months.
